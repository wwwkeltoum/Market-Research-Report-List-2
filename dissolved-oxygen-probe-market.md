<p><p>The Dissolved Oxygen Probe market consists of various types, with the most common being Galvanic Probe and Polarographic Probe. Galvanic probes operate by measuring the current produced when oxygen is consumed at a cathode, while Polarographic probes measure the oxygen concentration through a reduction-oxidation reaction at the cathode. Other types of probes include optical probes and luminescent probes which operate based on different principles. Each type of probe has its own advantages and applications in measuring dissolved oxygen in various settings.</p></p>
