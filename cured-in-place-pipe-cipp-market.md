<p><strong>The Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Inversion Type</li><li>Pull-in Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) is a trenchless repair method for deteriorated pipelines. There are two main types of CIPP installations: Inversion Type and Pull-in Type. In Inversion Type, a resin-saturated liner is inverted into the damaged pipe and then cured in place using hot water or steam. Pull-in Type involves pulling a liner into place using existing access points and then curing it in place. Both methods offer cost-effective and efficient solutions for rehabilitating underground pipelines.</p></p>
