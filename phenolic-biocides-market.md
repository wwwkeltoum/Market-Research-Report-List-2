<p><p>Phenolic biocides are utilized for their antimicrobial properties in a variety of industries. The market for phenolic biocides can be classified into three main types: nitrophenols, chlorophenols, and others. Nitrophenols are derivatives of phenol that contain a nitro group, while chlorophenols are phenols that have at least one chlorine atom attached. The "Others" category encompasses all other phenolic biocides that do not fall into the nitrophenols or chlorophenols subcategories. These phenolic biocides play a crucial role in inhibiting the growth of microorganisms and protecting products and materials from degradation.</p></p>
