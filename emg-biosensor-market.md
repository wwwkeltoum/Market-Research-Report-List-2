<p><p>EMG biosensors are devices used to measure electrical activity in muscles. Surface biosensors are placed on the skin to monitor muscle activity non-invasively, making them ideal for medical and sports applications. Intramuscular biosensors are implanted directly into muscles to provide more accurate and real-time data, suitable for research and clinical settings. Both types of EMG biosensors offer valuable insights into muscle performance, rehabilitation progress, and muscle-related disorders, contributing to advancements in healthcare and sports science.</p></p>
