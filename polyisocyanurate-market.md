<p><p>Polyisocyanurate (PIR) insulation can be categorized into two main market types based on their structure: flexibility and rigidity. Flexible PIR insulation is designed to provide thermal efficiency in curved or irregularly shaped spaces, making it ideal for applications where flexibility is required. On the other hand, rigid PIR insulation offers superior strength and durability, making it suitable for applications where structural support is essential. Both types of PIR insulation offer excellent thermal performance and contribute to energy efficiency in buildings.</p></p>
