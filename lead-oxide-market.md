<p><p>Lead oxide is commonly found in three forms in the market: Lead (II) Oxide, Lead (II, IV) Oxide, and Lead (IV) Oxide. Lead (II) Oxide, also known as lead monoxide, is a yellowish-red crystalline powder used in glass, ceramics, and plastic industries. Lead (II, IV) Oxide, or lead tetroxide, is a red or orange crystalline powder used as a pigment in paints and dyes. Lead (IV) Oxide, also known as lead dioxide, is a brown crystalline powder used in batteries and as a catalyst in organic reactions.</p></p>
